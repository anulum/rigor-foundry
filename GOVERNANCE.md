# Governance

## Authority

Miroslav Šotek is the project owner and final authority for architecture,
licensing, publication, release, visibility, and fleet activation. Maintainers
may implement and review within delegated claims; delegation does not transfer
release authority.

## Decision record

Material decisions require evidence, alternatives, consequences, and an
acceptance gate. Internal decision records remain in ignored internal or
monorepo coordination paths. Public architecture reflects accepted current
behaviour, not internal workflow narration.

## Change control

- Exact path claims prevent concurrent editing collisions.
- Authors and independent auditors are separate when two or more seats share a
  repository.
- Security, wire/schema, licensing, and publication changes require the
  applicable higher-risk review tier.
- Releases require a recorded quorum and explicit owner authorisation.
- Failed evidence and rejected candidates remain available for audit; history
  is not rewritten to hide them.

## Promotion

RigorFoundry becomes a fleet-wide audit authority only after its own promotion
contract passes and the owner activates it. Until then it is an audit candidate
system and cannot supersede repository-specific gates by implication.
